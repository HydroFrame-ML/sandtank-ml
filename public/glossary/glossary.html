Epoch:
One complete round of training and validation for a machine learning model. You can think of this as one training cycle.
L1:
This is one of the ways in which the accuracy of an ML model is calculated and refers to the sum of all the differences between the AI and simulation outputs. This can also be thought of as the total error measured (also known as total loss measured).
Loss:
A number indicating how bad the ML model’s prediction was. If the prediction is perfect, loss will be 0. The higher the loss, the greater the error.
LMax:
This is one way the accuracy of an ML model is calculated. It is the largest difference between what the AI predicted and the simulation output.
Permeability:
Permeability refers to the capacity of a geologic material (rock, gravel, sand, clay, etc.) to transmit fluids; the permeability of a material is mainly controlled by the size of pore spaces and how interconnected they are. High permeability material allows water/fluid to move through more easily than low permeability material. In the ParFlow Sandtank, there are a range of permeability values that go from low permeability material, like clay, to high permeability material, like gravel.
Machine Learning:
A type of artificial intelligence where computer systems learn and adapt without the benefit of explicit instructions. Instead they draw on algorithms and statistical models to analyze and draw inferences from patterns in data. The type of machine learning model utilized by Sandtank-ML is a neural network.
Machine Learning Output:
The prediction of saturation or pressure generated by the machine learning model based on the parameters, training set, and other variables selected.
Neural Network:
A computational learning system that uses a network of functions to understand and analyze data and produce an output. In neural networks, the ML system learns by processing large known datasets. This means that the datasets are labeled with the ‘correct’ answers. This enables the network to learn what characteristics of the input are needed to produce the correct output. Once a neural network has trained on enough known datasets, it can begin processing unknown datasets and return accurate results.
Physically Based Simulation:
A physically based simulation incorporates characteristics or properties of the systems they wish to model, which allows them to predict the behavior of that system when given specific inputs. In the physically based simulation step, we use a hydrology model that incorporates equations for: groundwater flow, or the movement of water underground; overland flow, the movement of water over the surface; and plant processes, like evapotranspiration. These equations allow us to simulate, or predict, how water will behave in our system.
Physically Based Simulation Output:
The prediction of saturation or pressure generated by the physically based simulation based on the parameters provided and the physical characteristics of the model area. Based on the inherent permeability of the materials and the user-defined boundary conditions, ParFlow will simulate an output. Output files are pressure and saturation, which the user can visualize separately. The pressure output (purple) shows how much pressure is present in each location in the model. Darker purple means greater pressure and lighter purple means lower pressure. The saturation output (blue) displays presence or absence of saturation; blue is saturation and white is unsaturated. Saturation:
Saturation refers to the amount of water in a geologic material. When all of the pore spaces and spaces between particles are filled with water, then a material is saturated. If there is a mix of water and air in those same spaces, then the material is unsaturated.
Standard Deviation:
Standard deviation is a common statistical term that refers to the measure of how spread out a set of numbers are. A low standard deviation means that most of the numbers are close to the mean (average) value; a high standard deviation means that the values are spread out over a wider range. In this case, the standard deviation is referring to the difference between the ML output and the simulation.
Training:
The process of optimizing your ML model by feeding it datasets to learn from.
Training Set:
The dataset you use to train your machine learning model. In Sandtank-ML, you get to choose between 3 different datasets: wet, dry, and all. The wet choice represents wet conditions (higher values), the dry choice represents dry conditions (low values), and the all choice includes both wet and dry datasets. Pay attention to how your choice impacts the accuracy of your model and if this changes depending on the initial conditions for the physically based simulation.
Validation:
the process where a trained model is evaluated with a testing data set. Through the process of validation, the places where the ML model is performing well and where there are high rates of errors are identified so that it can learn and improve through additional rounds of training and validation.
